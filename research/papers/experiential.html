Working Paper III · Experiential

Structural Insolvency

Metabolic Sovereignty and the Bio-Political Economy
of Autistic Burnout

Research Program › III. Experiential

Author: Ericwilliam Brown

Status: Under Review

Abstract

This paper examines the lived experience of sustained regulatory load — what it means to navigate a world not calibrated for your cognitive architecture. It links the energetic cost of compensatory regulation to intersectional identity, showing how the burden compounds across racialized, gendered, and neurological axes of normative categorization. The paper introduces the concept of structural insolvency: a state in which the metabolic debt of sustained mismatch exceeds the body's capacity to recover, resulting in autistic burnout.

Research Question

What does this cost the body?

Plain Language Summary

What does it actually feel like to navigate a world not built for your brain? This section explores the daily cost of constantly adjusting to fit in — and how that cost gets even higher when you're also dealing with racism, sexism, or other forms of discrimination. It's not just tiring; it's a kind of debt that builds up over years.

Position in the Research Program

This paper takes the computational model of energetic asymmetry from Paper II and examines its lived, phenomenological consequences. It provides the experiential evidence that the mechanistic pathway in Paper IV must explain — showing what sustained metabolic cost looks and feels like from the inside.
